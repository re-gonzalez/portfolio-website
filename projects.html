Hard Disk Drive Reaction Wheel

My first year with Space and Satellite Systems at UC Davis brought me the unique opportunity to work on the early stages of the hard disk drive (HDD) reaction wheel (RW) project. An initial study we conducted of the RW market found that 1.8 to 3.5 inch HDDs would provide similar angular momentum storage for a small fraction of the cost of industry-standard RWs. With these findings, we began to run simulations to evaluate performance metrics such as the torque and power consumption while working on used HDDss to control the angular velocity. The main goal of this project was to raise the technological readiness level of HDDs as reaction wheels.
